# The Great Expansion: The Era of Interplanetary Settlement

## Overview
From the perspective of a historian looking back at the "Early Expansion Era," the transition of humanity from a single-planet species to a multi-planetary civilization represents the most significant sociological and technological shift since the Neolithic Revolution. What began as competitive national prestige in the 20th century evolved into a synergistic global effort to ensure species survival and resource sustainability.

The colonization effort followed a logical progression of proximity and utility. The Moon served as the "Seventh Continent," a testing ground for closed-loop life support and low-gravity industry. Mars, however, became the true frontier—the first site of permanent, self-sustaining civilian governance. As these colonies matured, the focus shifted "beyond" to the asteroid belt and the moons of Jupiter and Saturn, transforming the solar system into a complex web of trade, mining, and scientific inquiry.

## Key Facts

### The Lunar Economy
The Moon was not colonized for living space, but for logistics. By utilizing lunar regolith for 3D-printing habitats and harvesting water ice from the poles, humanity created "gas stations in the sky." This drastically reduced the cost of deep-space travel by eliminating the need to launch heavy fuel from Earth's deep gravity well.

### Martian Sovereignty
Mars presented the first great political challenge of the future. Due to the communication lag (up to 24 minutes one way), Earth-based governance became impractical. This led to the *Martian Accords*, establishing a decentralized planetary government focused on terraforming and atmospheric thickening.

### The Outer Rim
Beyond Mars, colonization shifted toward "O'Neill Cylinders"—massive, rotating space stations that simulate gravity. These habitats became the primary residences for the mining guilds of the Asteroid Belt, creating a distinct "Belter" culture separate from both Earth and Mars.

## Did You Know?
* **Biological Divergence:** Due to lower gravity on Mars (38% of Earth's), second-generation Martian colonists are statistically taller and have lower bone density than their terrestrial ancestors.
* **The Water Gold-Rush:** In the early days of the Belt Rush, a single water-rich asteroid was valued more than the entire GDP of several small Earth nations.
* **The Silent Archive:** The Moon now houses the "Lunar Vault," a redundant backup of all human knowledge and DNA, designed to survive a planetary catastrophe on Earth.
