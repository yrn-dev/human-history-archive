# The Great Divergence: A History of the CRISPR Era

## Overview
Looking back from the dawn of the 22nd century, the "CRISPR Revolution" stands as the most pivotal inflection point in human biological history. What began in the early 21st century as a precise tool for curing monogenic diseases—such as sickle cell anemia and cystic fibrosis—rapidly evolved into a mechanism for elective human enhancement. 

The transition from *therapeutic* editing (fixing "broken" genes) to *augmentative* editing (adding "superior" traits) triggered a global socio-economic crisis known as the Great Divergence. For several decades, the ability to engineer cognitive capacity, physical longevity, and sensory perception was gated by wealth, creating a biological caste system. It was only through the Global Genetic Accord of 2084 that genomic editing was democratized and regulated, ensuring that the "Post-Human" transition did not result in the permanent fragmentation of the species.

## Key Facts

*   **The Shift to Germline Editing:** While early efforts focused on somatic cells (non-heritable), the shift to germline editing allowed changes to be passed to future generations. This effectively took the evolution of *Homo sapiens* out of the hands of natural selection and placed it into the hands of bio-engineers.
*   **Cognitive Augmentation:** By the 2050s, the identification of polygenic scores for intelligence allowed for the "optimization" of neural plasticity. This led to a surge in scientific productivity but also created immense psychological pressure on "enhanced" children to perform.
*   **The Longevity Leap:** The suppression of senescence genes (the biological clocks that cause aging) extended the average human healthy lifespan to 130 years. This necessitated a complete overhaul of global economics, retirement ages, and planetary population management.
*   **Ecological Backlash:** The use of "gene drives" to eliminate malaria-carrying mosquitoes caused unforeseen collapses in several tropical food chains, leading to the modern era of strict "Bio-Containment" laws.

## Did You Know?

1. **The "Naturalist" Movement:** In the 2070s, a significant cultural movement emerged where individuals refused all genetic edits, viewing the "unpredictability of nature" as the core of the human experience.
2. **Synthetic Senses:** Some early enhancements included the addition of tetrachromacy (the ability to see colors beyond the human spectrum) and infrared vision, borrowed from cephalopod and reptilian DNA.
3. **The Error Epoch:** Before the invention of "Prime-Editing 4.0," early enhancements often suffered from "off-target effects," where editing one gene accidentally triggered others, leading to the brief but terrifying "Mosaic Era" of unpredictable mutations.
