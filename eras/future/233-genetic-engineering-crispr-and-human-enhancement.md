# The Sculpted Genome: The Era of Directed Human Evolution

## Overview
From the perspective of the 23rd century, the "Genetic Transition" of the 21st and 22nd centuries stands as the most pivotal shift in anthropological history. The discovery and refinement of CRISPR-Cas9—and its subsequent successors, Prime Editing and Base Editing—marked the moment humanity ceased to be a product of random mutation and became the architect of its own biological blueprint.

What began as a noble effort to eradicate monogenic diseases, such as sickle cell anemia and cystic fibrosis, rapidly evolved into a broader movement toward "Human Enhancement." The boundary between therapy (healing the sick) and enhancement (improving the healthy) blurred as the socio-economic divide manifested biologically. The "Gen-Rich" elite began integrating cognitive boosters, increased bone density, and extended longevity markers into their germlines, creating a hereditary caste system that challenged the very definition of *Homo sapiens*. This era forced a global reckoning with bioethics, leading to the Great Genomic Accords of 2114, which established the universal right to genetic integrity.

## Key Facts

*   **Germline vs. Somatic Editing:** The primary conflict of the era centered on *somatic* editing (affecting only the patient) versus *germline* editing (changes passed to offspring). Germline editing was the catalyst for the "Divergence," as it allowed enhancements to become permanent familial traits.
*   **The Cognitive Leap:** By the mid-22nd century, "Neuro-Editing" allowed for the expansion of working memory and the acceleration of synaptic plasticity, effectively shortening the time required for complex education from decades to years.
*   **Biological Longevity:** Through the targeted suppression of senescence genes and the enhancement of telomere repair, the average human lifespan expanded from 80 years to approximately 150 years, fundamentally altering global economics and retirement structures.
*   **The Ecological Ripple:** Human enhancement did not happen in a vacuum; the same tools were used to "de-extinct" keystone species and engineer crops that could thrive in the volatile climates of the late 21st century.

## Did You Know?

1. **The "Silent" Generation:** There exists a generation of humans born during the transition who are "mosaics"—possessing a mix of edited and unedited cells due to early, imperfect CRISPR delivery systems.
2. **Genetic Art:** In the 22nd century, a brief trend emerged where wealthy patrons edited their skin to exhibit bioluminescence or shifting pigment patterns, treating the human body as a living canvas.
3. **The Memory Archive:** Some early enhancers attempted to "hard-code" ancestral knowledge into non-coding DNA, though this proved unstable and largely failed, leading to the development of external neural interfaces instead.
