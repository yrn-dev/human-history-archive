# The Sculpted Genome: The Era of Human Enhancement

## Overview
Looking back from the vantage point of the 22nd century, the "Genetic Revolution" stands as the most profound pivot point in human evolution. What began in the early 21st century as a modest tool for curing monogenic diseases—CRISPR-Cas9—evolved into a comprehensive architectural suite for the human biological form. 

For millennia, human evolution was a slow, stochastic process driven by natural selection. The advent of precision germline editing shifted this paradigm from *chance* to *choice*. The transition from "therapeutic editing" (fixing mutations) to "enhancement editing" (augmenting capabilities) sparked the Great Bioethical Debates of the 2040s, eventually leading to the establishment of the Global Genomic Accord. Today, the distinction between "natural" and "enhanced" has largely blurred, as cognitive boosts, extended longevity, and metabolic optimization have become standard markers of the modern human experience.

## Key Facts

*   **The Therapy-Enhancement Divide:** Early legislation strictly forbade "enhancement." However, the line vanished when "preventative editing" (e.g., editing genes to prevent Alzheimer's) inadvertently resulted in superior memory and cognitive processing.
*   **Socio-Genetic Stratification:** The mid-century was marked by "Gen-Gaps," where wealth determined access to enhancements. This led to the 2062 Universal Genetic Access Act, which codified genomic health as a fundamental human right to prevent a biological caste system.
*   **Epigenetic Tuning:** Modern enhancement is no longer just about cutting DNA. We now utilize "Epigenetic Switches," allowing individuals to toggle certain traits (like heightened sensory perception or rapid muscle recovery) on or off depending on their environment.
*   **Biodiversity Loss:** Historians note a paradoxical decline in genetic diversity. As parents selected for "optimal" traits (height, intelligence, temperament), the human gene pool narrowed, requiring the creation of the Global Seed & Soma Vault to preserve ancestral wild-type DNA.
