# The Great Pyramid of Giza: Engineering the Eternal

The Great Pyramid of Giza, the sole survivor of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, stands as a testament to the organizational genius and architectural ambition of the Old Kingdom of Egypt. Built for the Pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BCE, this colossal structure was the tallest man-made object on Earth for over 3,800 years. For historians and archaeologists, the pyramid is not merely a tomb, but a complex puzzle of mathematics, astronomy, and logistics.

### The Enigma of Construction

The primary mystery surrounding the Great Pyramid is not *why* it was built, but *how*. Composed of approximately 2.3 million stone blocks—some weighing up to 80 tons—the precision of its alignment is staggering. The pyramid is oriented to true north with an accuracy of within three-sixtieths of a degree.

While popular culture often suggests the use of slave labor, modern archaeological evidence from the "Workers' Village" suggests a highly organized workforce of skilled laborers and conscripted peasants. These workers were well-fed and housed, viewing their labor as a form of national service or religious duty.

As for the lifting of the stones, historians debate several theories. The most widely accepted is the use of a sophisticated system of ramps—either linear, zigzagging, or internal spirals—combined with sledges and lubricated tracks of wet Nile clay to reduce friction. The internal "Grand Gallery" may have served as a counterweight system to help hoist the massive granite slabs used to seal the King’s Chamber.

### Key Facts

*   **Materials:** The core consists of local yellow limestone, while the interior chambers used massive Aswan granite. Originally, the entire structure was encased in polished white Tura limestone, which would have made the pyramid shine brilliantly under the sun.
*   **Dimensions:** Originally standing at 146.6 meters (481 feet), it consists of a base covering roughly 13 acres.
*   **Internal Layout:** Unlike most pyramids, it contains three known chambers: the King's Chamber, the Queen's Chamber, and an unfinished subterranean chamber.

### Did You Know?

1. **The Missing Casing:** Most of the smooth, white outer casing stones were stripped away during the Middle Ages to build mosques and fortresses in Cairo.
2. **Thermal Stability:** Despite the scorching Egyptian heat, the temperature inside the Great Pyramid remains constant at approximately 20 degrees Celsius (68 degrees Fahrenheit).
3. **Mathematical Precision:** The perimeter of the pyramid's base divided by twice its height is roughly equal to $\pi$ (pi), suggesting the Egyptians had a sophisticated understanding of circular geometry.
