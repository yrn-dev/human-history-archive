# The Great Pyramid of Giza: Engineering the Eternal

The Great Pyramid of Giza, commissioned by the Pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BCE, remains the most iconic testament to the ingenuity of the Old Kingdom of Egypt. As the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza plateau, it stood as the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years. For historians and archaeologists, the monument is not merely a tomb, but a complex puzzle of mathematics, astronomy, and logistics.

### Construction Methods and Theories
The sheer scale of the pyramid—consisting of roughly 2.3 million limestone and granite blocks—defies simple explanation. While popular myths often suggest slave labor, archaeological evidence from the nearby "Worker’s Village" indicates a highly organized workforce of paid skilled laborers and seasonal farmers.

The primary mystery lies in the lifting mechanism. The most widely accepted theory is the use of a **ramp system**. Scholars debate whether these were straight ramps, zig-zagging paths, or internal spiral ramps that allowed workers to haul massive stones upward. The precision is staggering; the base is level to within a few centimeters, and the sides are aligned almost perfectly to the four cardinal points of the compass.

Inside, the structure is equally sophisticated. The Grand Gallery and the King’s Chamber utilize massive granite slabs, some weighing up to 80 tons, transported from Aswan—over 500 miles away. The method used to hoist these beams to such heights remains a subject of intense academic debate.

### Key Facts
*   **Original Height:** Approximately 146.6 meters (481 feet), now slightly shorter due to the loss of its pyramidion (capstone).
*   **Material:** Primarily local limestone, with fine white Tura limestone for the outer casing and red granite for the interior chambers.
*   **Alignment:** The pyramid is aligned to true north with an accuracy of within three-sixtieths of a degree.
*   **Purpose:** Designed as a *mer* (place of ascension) to facilitate the Pharaoh's journey to the afterlife.

### Did You Know?
1. **The Missing Casing:** The pyramid was once covered in polished white limestone that reflected the sun like a mirror, making it visible from miles away. Most of this was stripped away in the Middle Ages to build mosques and palaces in Cairo.
2. **Internal Vents:** The "air shafts" leading from the King and Queen's chambers are believed by some astronomers to be aligned with specific stars, such as Orion and Sirius, to guide the Pharaoh's soul.
3. **Temperature Control:** Despite the scorching Egyptian heat, the temperature inside the Great Pyramid remains constant at approximately 20 degrees Celsius (68 degrees Fahrenheit).
