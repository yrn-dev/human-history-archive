# Ancient Egypt: Cradle of Civilization

## Overview

Ancient Egyptian civilization emerged along the Nile River around 3100 BCE and lasted until 30 BCE.

## Timeline

| Period | Dates | Key Pharaohs |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Early Dynastic | 3100-2686 BCE | Narmer, Aha |
| Old Kingdom | 2686-2181 BCE | Djoser, Khufu |
| Middle Kingdom | 2055-1650 BCE | Mentuhotep, Amenemhat |
| New Kingdom | 1550-1069 BCE | Hatshepsut, Ramesses II |
| Late Period | 664-332 BCE | Psamtik, Nectanebo |

## Major Achievements

- **Pyramids of Giza** (~2560 BCE)
- **Hieroglyphic writing** (~3200 BCE)
- **Medicine** (surgery, anatomy)
- **Mathematics** (fractions, geometry)
- **Architecture** (temples, tombs)

## Daily Life

- Agriculture along the Nile
- Social hierarchy (Pharaoh → priests → scribes → peasants)
- Religion and the afterlife
- Trade with neighboring regions
