# The Great Pyramid of Giza: Engineering the Eternal

The Great Pyramid of Giza, the last remaining Wonder of the Ancient World, stands as a testament to the organizational genius and architectural ambition of the Old Kingdom of Egypt. Built for the Pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BCE, this colossal structure was the tallest man-made object on Earth for over 3,800 years. To the modern historian, it is not merely a tomb, but a masterclass in mathematics, logistics, and social mobilization.

### The Mystery of Construction
The primary debate surrounding the pyramid centers on *how* 2.3 million limestone and granite blocks—some weighing up to 80 tons—were transported and lifted. While the "ramp theory" remains the most widely accepted, scholars debate whether these ramps were linear, zigzagged, or internal spirals. 

Recent discoveries suggest a sophisticated logistical network; the Egyptians likely utilized the Nile’s seasonal flooding to float massive stones from Tura and Aswan directly to the Giza plateau via man-made canals. Furthermore, the precision of the pyramid's alignment to true north is staggering, with an error of only a fraction of a degree, suggesting an advanced understanding of stellar observation.

### Key Facts
*   **Composition:** The core consists of local yellow limestone, while the interior chambers used massive granite slabs. The exterior was originally covered in "casing stones" of polished Tura limestone that reflected the sun.
*   **Labor Force:** Contrary to the myth of slave labor, archaeological evidence from the "Workers' Village" suggests a highly organized workforce of paid laborers and skilled artisans who were well-fed and respected.
*   **Internal Architecture:** The pyramid contains three known chambers: the King's Chamber, the Queen's Chamber, and the unfinished Subterranean Chamber, connected by the enigmatic Grand Gallery.

### Did You Know?
1. **The Missing Capstone:** The pyramid originally ended in a *pyramidion*, a capstone likely covered in gold or electrum to catch the first rays of the sun.
2. **Eight Sides, Not Four:** From the ground, the pyramid looks four-sided, but from the air during an equinox, a slight concavity in the faces reveals that it actually has eight sides.
3. **Precision Fit:** The joints between the casing stones were so precise that, in some areas, a razor blade cannot fit between them.
