# The Great Pyramid of Giza: Engineering the Eternal

The Great Pyramid of Giza, built for the Pharaoh Khufu during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom, remains one of the most formidable achievements of human ingenuity. Standing as the only surviving member of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, it served not merely as a tomb, but as a cosmic statement of the Pharaoh's divine authority and his journey into the afterlife. For millennia, historians and engineers have grappled with how a Bronze Age society achieved such mathematical precision and structural longevity.

### Construction Methods and Mysteries

The construction of the Great Pyramid involved the quarrying and placement of approximately 2.3 million stone blocks, some weighing up to 80 tons. While the core consists of local limestone, the exterior was originally encased in polished Tura limestone, which would have reflected the sun with a blinding brilliance.

The primary mystery lies in the **transport and elevation** of these stones. While the "internal ramp" theory and the "spiral ramp" theory are widely debated among archaeologists, the sheer logistics of moving massive granite slabs from Aswan—over 500 miles away—suggests a highly sophisticated mastery of the Nile's flood cycles and barge transport.

Furthermore, the pyramid's **astronomical alignment** is staggering. Its four sides are aligned almost perfectly with the cardinal points of the compass, with a margin of error of only a fraction of a degree. This implies that the Egyptians possessed an advanced understanding of stellar observation long before the advent of modern navigational tools.

Inside, the structure deviates from the standard pyramid layout. The presence of the Grand Gallery and the "Air Shafts" leading from the King's Chamber toward specific constellations (such as Orion and Thuban) suggests the pyramid was a machine for the soul, designed to launch the Pharaoh's spirit into the circumpolar stars.

### Key Facts
*   **Original Height:** Approximately 146.6 meters (481 feet).
*   **Labor Force:** Contrary to popular myth, evidence suggests the pyramid was built by paid skilled laborers and farmers during the off-season, not slaves.
*   **Precision:** The base is level to within 15 millimeters across its entire 13-acre footprint.

### Did You Know?
1. **The Casing Stones:** The pyramid was once covered in smooth, white limestone that acted like a mirror; most of these were stripped away in the Middle Ages to build mosques and fortresses in Cairo.
2. **Thermal Stability:** Despite the scorching heat of the Sahara, the temperature inside the King's Chamber remains a constant 20 degrees Celsius (68°F).
3. **The Mortar Mystery:** The mortar used to bind the stones is stronger than the stones themselves, and its exact chemical composition remains only partially understood by modern scientists.
