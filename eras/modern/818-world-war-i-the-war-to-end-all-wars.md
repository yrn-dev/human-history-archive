# World War I: The War to End All Wars

## Overview
World War I, known at the time as "The Great War," was a global catastrophe that reshaped the geopolitical landscape of the 20th century. Lasting from 1914 to 1918, it represented a collision between 19th-century military tactics and 20th-century industrial technology. What began as a regional conflict in the Balkans quickly escalated into a total war, drawing in the world's great powers.

The war was fought primarily between two opposing coalitions: the **Allied Powers** (led by France, Great Britain, Russia, Italy, and later the United States) and the **Central Powers** (led by Germany, Austria-Hungary, and the Ottoman Empire). The conflict is most infamous for the stalemate of trench warfare on the Western Front, where millions of soldiers endured horrific conditions for negligible territorial gains. By the time the Armistice was signed on November 11, 1918, the war had dismantled four great empires and left an estimated 16 to 20 million people dead, leaving a legacy of trauma and political instability that would eventually lead to World War II.

## Key Facts
*   **Industrialized Slaughter:** WWI saw the first widespread use of tanks, airplanes, submarines (U-boats), and machine guns, which made traditional cavalry charges obsolete and deadly.
*   **Total War:** The conflict was a "total war," meaning nations mobilized their entire populations. Women entered the workforce in unprecedented numbers to support the war effort, accelerating the movement for women's suffrage.
*   **The Collapse of Empires:** The war resulted in the fall of the German, Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, and Russian Empires, leading to the creation of new nations such as Poland, Czechoslovakia, and Yugoslavia.
*   **The League of Nations:** In an attempt to prevent future conflicts, the Treaty of Versailles established the League of Nations, the precursor to the modern United Nations.

## Did You Know?
1. **The Christmas Truce:** In December 1914, soldiers from both the British and German sides spontaneously ceased fire to sing carols, exchange gifts, and even play soccer in "No Man's Land."
2. **Animal Heroes:** Millions of animals served in the war, including dogs used for messengers and sentinel work, and pigeons that delivered critical messages through enemy lines.
3. **The "Spanish Flu":** The end of the war coincided with one of the deadliest pandemics in history. It was called the "Spanish Flu" not because it started in Spain, but because Spain was neutral and was the only country honestly reporting the death tolls.
