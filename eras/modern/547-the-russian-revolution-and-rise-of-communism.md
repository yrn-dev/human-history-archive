# The Red Storm: The Russian Revolution and the Rise of Communism

## Overview
The Russian Revolution of 1917 was not a single event, but a series of seismic political shifts that dismantled the centuries-old Romanov autocracy and birthed the world's first socialist state. Driven by systemic inequality, a bankrupt economy, and the catastrophic toll of World War I, the revolution transitioned Russia from a feudal empire to the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR).

The process unfolded in two primary stages. The **February Revolution** saw spontaneous protests in Petrograd lead to the abdication of Tsar Nicholas II, replacing the monarchy with a fragile Provisional Government. However, this government failed to exit the war or address land hunger among the peasantry. This vacuum allowed Vladimir Lenin and the Bolsheviks—a radical faction of the Social Democrats—to seize power in the **October Revolution**. By promising "Peace, Land, and Bread," the Bolsheviks implemented a Marxist-Leninist framework, aiming to abolish private property and establish a classless society. The subsequent Russian Civil War (1917–1923) solidified Communist control, setting the stage for a global ideological struggle that would define the 20th century.

## Key Facts
*   **Ideological Root:** The revolution was based on the theories of Karl Marx, who argued that the working class (proletariat) must overthrow the capitalist class (bourgeoisie) to end exploitation.
*   **The Role of WWI:** The war acted as a catalyst. Millions of Russian soldiers died, and inflation skyrocketed, making the Tsarist regime appear incompetent and indifferent to human suffering.
*   **The Red Terror:** To consolidate power and eliminate "class enemies," the Bolsheviks established the Cheka (secret police), initiating a period of mass arrests and executions.
*   **Economic Shift:** Under communism, the state took control of all means of production, replacing market capitalism with a centrally planned economy.

## Did You Know?
1. **The Romanov End:** After the revolution, Tsar Nicholas II and his entire immediate family were executed in a basement in Yekaterinburg to prevent them from becoming a rallying point for royalist forces.
2. **The Calendar Gap:** Russia used the Julian calendar at the time. This is why the "October Revolution" actually took place in November according to the Gregorian calendar used in the West.
3. **Lenin's Preservation:** To maintain his status as an eternal symbol of the revolution, Vladimir Lenin's body was embalmed and has remained on public display in a mausoleum in Red Square since 1924.
