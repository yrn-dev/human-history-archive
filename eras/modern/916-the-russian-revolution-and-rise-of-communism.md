# The Russian Revolution and the Rise of Communism

## Overview
The Russian Revolution of 1917 was not a single event, but a series of seismic political upheavals that dismantled the centuries-old Romanov autocracy and birthed the world's first socialist state. Driven by systemic inequality, the devastation of World War I, and a starving peasantry, Russia transitioned from a feudal empire to the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR).

The revolution unfolded in two primary stages. The **February Revolution** saw the collapse of Tsar Nicholas II’s regime, replacing it with a fragile Provisional Government. However, this government failed to address the public's demand for "Peace, Land, and Bread." This vacuum allowed Vladimir Lenin and the Bolsheviks—a radical faction of the Social Democratic Labour Party—to seize power during the **October Revolution**. By implementing Marxist-Leninist ideology, the Bolsheviks sought to abolish private property and establish a "dictatorship of the proletariat," fundamentally altering the global geopolitical landscape for the remainder of the 20th century.

## Key Facts

*   **The Catalyst of WWI:** Russia's involvement in World War I was disastrous. Millions of soldiers died, and the domestic economy collapsed, leading to hyperinflation and acute food shortages in cities like Petrograd.
*   **Lenin’s Vision:** Vladimir Lenin adapted Karl Marx's theories to the Russian context, arguing that a disciplined "vanguard party" was necessary to lead the workers toward revolution, rather than waiting for capitalism to collapse naturally.
*   **The Red Terror:** To consolidate power during the Civil War, the Bolsheviks employed the *Cheka* (secret police) to eliminate "class enemies" and political dissidents through mass arrests and executions.
*   **Shift to Totalitarianism:** While the revolution began with promises of worker councils (*Soviets*), the system quickly evolved into a highly centralized bureaucracy. Under Joseph Stalin, this culminated in a totalitarian regime characterized by forced collectivization and the Great Purge.

## Did You Know?

1. **The Calendar Gap:** The "October Revolution" actually took place in November according to the Gregorian calendar; Russia was still using the Julian calendar at the time.
2. **The Royal Fate:** Tsar Nicholas II and his entire immediate family were executed in a basement in Yekaterinburg in 1918 to prevent them from becoming a rallying point for the White Army.
3. **The Sealed Train:** Germany actively helped Lenin return to Russia from exile in 1917, transporting him in a "sealed train" in hopes that he would destabilize the Russian war effort and force Russia out of WWI.
