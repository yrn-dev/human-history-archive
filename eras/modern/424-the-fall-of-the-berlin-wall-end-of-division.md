# The Fall of the Berlin Wall: The End of Division

## Overview
For twenty-eight years, the Berlin Wall stood as the most potent physical symbol of the Cold War. Erected in 1961 by the German Democratic Republic (GDR), the wall did more than divide a city; it bifurcated the world into two opposing ideological camps: the capitalist West and the communist East. 

By the late 1980s, the geopolitical landscape began to shift. The Soviet Union, under the leadership of Mikhail Gorbachev, introduced policies of *glasnost* (openness) and *perestroika* (restructuring), signaling a reduction in Soviet control over its satellite states. Simultaneously, a wave of peaceful protests known as the "Peaceful Revolution" swept through East Germany, as citizens demanded democratic reforms and freedom of movement.

The climax occurred on the evening of November 9, 1989. Following a botched announcement by GDR official Günter Schabowski—who mistakenly suggested that travel restrictions were being lifted "immediately"—thousands of East Berliners flocked to the checkpoints. Overwhelmed and without clear orders, border guards opened the gates. In an explosion of joy, citizens from both sides climbed the wall, hacking away at the concrete with hammers and chisels, effectively ending the division of Germany and signaling the imminent collapse of the Iron Curtain.

## Timeline of the Collapse

## Key Facts
*   **The "Death Strip":** The wall was not just one barrier, but a complex system including two walls separated by a "death strip" containing landmines, guard dogs, and watchtowers.
*   **Casualties:** While exact numbers are debated, it is estimated that at least 140 people died attempting to cross the wall between 1961 and 1989.
*   **Geopolitical Ripple Effect:** The fall of the wall acted as a catalyst for the revolutions of 1989 across Eastern Europe, leading to the dissolution of the Warsaw Pact and, eventually, the Soviet Union in 1991.
*   **The "Mauerspechte":** The people who climbed the wall to chip away pieces of concrete as souvenirs were nicknamed *Mauerspechte* (wall woodpeckers).

## Did You Know?
1. **A Mistake Changed History:** The wall fell largely due to a bureaucratic error. Günter Schabowski had not been fully briefed on the new travel rules and misspoke during a live press conference, triggering the rush to the border.
2. **The Wall Had a "Secret" Side:** The wall was designed to keep people *in*, not keep people *out*. The fortifications, alarms, and traps were almost entirely oriented toward the East.
3. **The Wall Still Exists (In Pieces):** While most was demolished, sections remain as memorials, most notably the East Side Gallery, where artists from around the world painted murals on the remaining concrete.
