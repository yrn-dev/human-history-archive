# The Great Depression: The Decade of Global Despair

## Overview
The Great Depression was the most severe economic downturn in the history of the industrialized modern world, lasting roughly from 1929 to 1939. While often associated with the dramatic imagery of breadlines and the "Dust Bowl," the Depression was a complex systemic failure triggered by a combination of unsustainable credit expansion, agricultural overproduction, and a catastrophic collapse of the financial markets.

The crisis began in the United States but rapidly metastasized into a global phenomenon due to the interconnected nature of international trade and war debts from World War I. As banks failed and currencies collapsed, unemployment soared—reaching nearly 25% in the U.S.—and global GDP plummeted. The era was defined by a fundamental shift in the role of government; the prevailing *laissez-faire* philosophy of the 1920s gave way to the "New Deal" in America and similar state-led interventions globally, establishing the modern social safety net.

## Timeline of Collapse and Recovery

| Year | Key Event | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1929** | Black Tuesday (Oct 29) | The stock market crash wipes out billions in wealth. |
| **1930** | Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act | Protectionist trade barriers stifle global commerce. |
| **1932** | Banking Crisis Peaks | Thousands of banks fail; life savings vanish for millions. |
| **1933** | The New Deal Begins | FDR introduces massive public works and financial reform. |
| **1935** | Social Security Act | Establishment of old-age pensions and unemployment insurance. |
| **1939** | Outbreak of WWII | Massive military spending finally ends the depression. |

## Key Facts

*   **The Banking Domino Effect:** The crisis was exacerbated by "bank runs," where panicked depositors attempted to withdraw their money simultaneously. Because banks held only a fraction of deposits in reserve, this led to a total collapse of the credit system.
*   **The Dust Bowl:** Environmental catastrophe struck the American Great Plains. Severe drought and poor farming practices led to massive dust storms, forcing hundreds of thousands of "Okies" and "Arkies" to migrate west toward California.
*   **Deflationary Spiral:** Unlike modern inflation, the Depression was characterized by *deflation*. Prices dropped sharply, which sounds beneficial but actually discouraged spending and investment, as people waited for prices to fall further, further stalling the economy.
*   **Global Reach:** Germany was hit particularly hard, as the cessation of American loans under the Dawes Plan crippled their economy, creating the political instability that contributed to the rise of the Nazi Party.

## Did You Know?

1. **The "Hoovervilles":** Shanty towns built by homeless people were named "Hoovervilles" after President Herbert Hoover, whom the public blamed for the government's perceived inaction.
2. **Radio's Golden Age:** Despite the poverty, the 1930s saw a boom in radio ownership. "Fireside Chats" by FDR used this medium to speak directly to the public, calming national panic.
3. **The 25-Cent Steak:** During the height of the depression, some restaurants offered "depression specials" and extremely low prices just to attract any customer who had a few coins to spend.
